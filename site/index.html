Build better. Then close the laptop.

Plan-driven development for Claude Code.

The problem

AI-assisted development is a superpower. It's also a trap. You finish in an hour what used to take a day — then fill the remaining seven hours with more work. The productivity gains disappear into an ever-expanding backlog.

Nobody ships faster by working longer. They ship faster by getting it right the first time.

Flowers are cheap. Divorce is expensive. — Ben Horowitz, The Hard Thing About Hard Things

What plankit does

Plans that direct Claude toward predictable, correct results. Guidelines that prevent the nightmare sessions. Skills that codify your workflow so Claude doesn't rediscover your project every conversation.

  • Plan Think before building. Claude proposes, you direct, then execute with confidence.
  • Guard Battle-tested guidelines that prevent scope creep, silent fallbacks, and shortcuts without permission.
  • Ship Changelog and release skills that close the loop. One command to tag. One command to push.

The real value

You're already more productive than you've ever been. You don't have to fill every recovered hour with more output.

Your kid wants to show you something. Your partner asked you a question. Your friend texted an hour ago.

plankit helps you finish the work — so you can do the thing that actually matters.

Get started

go install github.com/markwharton/plankit/cmd/pk@latest
pk setup

Two commands. Then read the documentation or just start building.
